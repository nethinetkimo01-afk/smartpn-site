Material identity

Different Systems, One Material Language

Each role in the manufacturing-side supply chain can keep using its own system. The communication gap closes when the same material carries the same shared identity.

Different systems should not mean different material meanings.

A brand may use one ERP. An OEM may use a PDM or PLM. A supplier may maintain material data in its own internal system.

These systems do not need to become one system. But when they refer to the same material, they need a shared material language.

Where the gap starts

In footwear and apparel, the same material can become different records across supplier, OEM, and brand systems. One supplier material name becomes one OEM code, then another brand-side material ID.

This is not because companies lack systems. Most already have systems. The problem is that those systems do not share the same material identity.

What SmartPN Atlas adds

SmartPN Atlas is built for this layer: a shared material identity layer for the manufacturing-side supply chain.

It is not another ERP. It is not another PDM or PLM. It is not a central database that forces every company into the same environment.

It gives one material a shared reference so supplier, OEM, and brand can keep their own systems while reducing the communication gap between them.

Why it matters

When material identity is shared, downstream work becomes easier: sourcing comparison, supplier alternatives, BOM reference, traceability output, ESG support, and DPP readiness all become more credible because the source data is easier to trust.

Same material. Same meaning. Across the manufacturing-side supply chain.
