Habit Does Not Mean It Is Right

The industry has normalized the same material existing under different names, codes and versions, but normalization does not mean the structure is right.

Habit does not mean it is right.

Brands, OEMs, and suppliers have become used to rebuilding their own material libraries. The same material is renamed, recoded, and rechecked again and again.

This is the standard-zero zone: many systems, many records, but no shared material language.
